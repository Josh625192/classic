Statistics of the Classic

    The U.S. pitching staff as a whole allowed only two earned runs over its final 26 innings, bringing its final ERA to 2.15.
    WBC 2017 set the attendance record for the tournament, with a total announced attendance of 1,086,720. It was the first time in the four Classics that attendance surpassed the 1 million mark. That included a whopping 51,565 at Dodger Stadium for the finals.
    Going into the championship game, Puerto Rico led the tournament in both runs scored and ERA (2.29), outscoring opponents by a margin of 55-18.
    In his last game as a manager, Team USA’s Jim Leyland walked off the field a champion.
    There was a 34% attendance increase for round one (621,851) compared to 2013.
    The World Baseball Classic Championship Game was the largest ever U.S. audience for a Spanish language telecast of a baseball game with 761,000 viewers on ESPN Deportes and a simulcast on ESPN2.
